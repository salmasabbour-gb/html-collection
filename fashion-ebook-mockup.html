Context

Fashion market observations

Eight market observations on how shoppers now choose, switch, and stay, each backed by recent independent data. Price has overtaken familiarity, and loyalty built on habit alone no longer holds.

Shoppers are choosing cheaper options

64%chose a cheaper option

Clothes are easy to cut back on, so when money is tight they go first. Price now matters more than brand habit.

McKinsey × BoF, State of Fashion

Dupe culture has gone mainstream

67%buy dupes to save money

A near-identical product at a fraction of the price is now the goal, not a compromise.

Morning Consult, 2025

Loyalty fades fastest among the young

29%of Gen Z's wardrobe is one brand

Versus 52% for over-50s, and half of Gen Z prefer to explore new brands. Your name alone will not keep them.

McKinsey × BoF, State of Fashion

Values matter, but skepticism is high

57%distrust most "green" claims

Only about a third will pay more for sustainable products. Proof beats slogans.

Deloitte, The Sustainable Consumer

Traditional marketing is losing its grip

2.4×more authentic: real-customer vs brand content

Shoppers trust real customers and word of mouth more than brand or influencer ads.

SoPost; Edelman Trust Barometer

Resale is reshaping the category

$393Bglobal secondhand apparel market

About 10% of all apparel spend and growing fast among younger shoppers. Loyalty now competes with resale.

ThredUp / GlobalData Resale Report

Returns quietly eat fashion's profits

20–40%apparel return rate

Well above the ~16% retail average. Rewarding customers who keep what they buy protects margin and carbon.

NRF, Retail Returns Landscape

Shoppers now expect personalization

71%expect personalized experiences

And 76% are frustrated without them. Personalization also lifts repeat purchase.

McKinsey, Next in Personalization

02
Problem

Where loyalty goes wrong

The myths fashion teams still believe, the tactics they produce, and the reality.

The myth
The tactic it produces
The reality
"A loyalty program means points."
Points-only schemes
Points reward spending you already had. Without engagement, status, or community, they rarely change behavior.
"Discounts build loyalty."
Constant promotions and sales
Discounts train shoppers to wait for the next markdown, eroding margin and brand value.
"Everyone should get the same reward."
One-size-fits-all rewards
Identical rewards over-reward bargain hunters and under-reward your real fans.
"Gen Z can't be loyal."
Writing off younger shoppers
Gen Z is loyal to brands that earn it through values, community, and experience.
"Fashion is too seasonal for loyalty."
Treating each drop as a one-off
Seasonality is a reason to build a year-round relationship, not an excuse to skip one.
"An influencer push is engagement."
Influencer-only activation
Borrowed audiences leave when the campaign ends. Owned engagement compounds.
"Online and in-store loyalty are separate."
Channel-siloed programs
Shoppers move fluidly across channels. A split program breaks both the experience and the data.
Reality check Loyalty is a multiplier, not a cure

A loyalty program rewards and compounds a good experience. It can't rescue a weak product, slow shipping, or poor service. Loyalty is one piece of a full ecosystem (product, service, post-purchase support, brand). Fix those basics first, and loyalty makes good customers come back more often.

Best practices

What to do instead

The moves that turn one-time buyers into repeat customers. Use them as a checklist for your loyalty program.

01

Reward engagement, not just spend

Recognize the behaviors that build a relationship (reviews, referrals, content, repeat visits), not only transactions.

02

Make status and progress feel earned

Tiers, milestones, and challenges give shoppers a reason to return between purchases, which matters most in a seasonal category.

03

Lead with value and authenticity, then prove it

Tie rewards to what shoppers actually care about, and back every claim with evidence rather than slogans.

04

Build owned relationships, reduce reliance on borrowed reach

Turn customers into your channel through referrals, community, and first-party data.

05

Be channel-agnostic by design

One identity, one balance, one experience across web, app, and store, whatever platform you run on.

06

Reward profitable loyalty, not just spend

In a high-return category, recognize the customers who buy and keep. For example, a tier for high-spend, low-return shoppers turns retention into margin, not just revenue.

Fashion use case Turn returns into a loyalty lever

High return rates drain margin, add operational complexity, and raise a brand's carbon footprint. If you capture each customer's return rate as loyalty data, you can (1) segment customers to see who returns at high rates, and (2) reward high-spend, low-return shoppers for their efficient, lower-waste buying. The same program now protects margin and supports sustainability goals, not just repeat revenue.

Proof

Success stories

Nike logo

Nike

Membership + gamification

Membership and app-based gamification (Nike Run Club, SNKRS) deepen engagement and push sales toward Nike's own direct channels, which sit around 40% of total revenue. Built in-house, not on a third-party platform.

adidas logo

adidas adiClub

Four engagement tiers

Members earn by logging workouts, writing reviews, and attending events, not just by buying. Bond's 2024 Loyalty Report named it the top apparel program, and found loyalty members about 4x more likely to spend more and 2x more likely to advocate.

The North Face logo

The North Face

Access, not discounts

Points and tiers paired with early access to drops, exclusive events, and an extended-returns perk. Loyalty built on experience, not markdowns.

Gymshark logo

Gymshark

Community over discounts

Built a billion-dollar brand on community, not markdowns. Its 66-day fitness challenge turns customers into a movement, and its program rewards workouts and engagement, not just spend.

Recommendations

Your next steps

Keep customers by building a real relationship with them, not by being the cheapest. Anyone can copy a discount, so price alone won't keep them loyal.

Reward more than spending. Give customers perks and status for engaging, not just for buying.

Win younger shoppers with shared values, community, and evidence that backs up your claims, not just marketing slogans.

Build an audience you own (email and SMS subscribers, app users, loyalty members) and grow it through referrals, so you rely less on paid ads.

Give customers the same loyalty experience everywhere they shop: online, in-app, and in-store.
